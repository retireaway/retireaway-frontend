### Visas and Residency

- **[Official Italian Visa Portal](https://vistoperitalia.esteri.it/?lang=en_US)**

Regardless of the visa type, all foreign nationals must apply for a residence permit (permesso di soggiorno) within eight working days of entering Italy to formalize their legal status. While holders of a Type D visa can circulate within the Schengen Area for up to 90 days in any 180-day period, they must still comply with local registration laws in each member state they visit.

For those looking to work locally, the Self-Employment Visa is available but often restricted by the Decreto Flussi, a government decree that sets annual quotas for foreign workers. Regardless of the category, once a visa is granted and you enter the country, you must apply for a Permesso di Soggiorno (residence permit) at a local post office within eight days of your arrival.
